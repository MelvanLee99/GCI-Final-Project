# GCI Final Project
GCI Final Project
A GCI Final Assignment for completion of Global Consumer Intelligence Course 2025. Our purpose of this project to addressed critical HR challenges including limited advancement opportunities, poor work-life balance, and ineffective leadership — identified as primary drivers of employee attrition. Led a data-driven initiative to analyze and predict employee attrition across consumer industries, leveraging machine learning to help organizations reduce turnover and improve long-term workforce planning.

What I done was:

- Analyzed a dataset of 1,470 employees with attributes including job role, tenure, monthly income, performance metrics, and training history.

- Conducted exploratory data analysis (EDA) and data visualization to uncover trends — identifying that junior employees and those with inadequate incentives were most at risk.

- Addressed class imbalance using resampling techniques to improve model accuracy.

- Built and compared three machine learning models: SVM, k-NN, and Random Forest.

- Achieved 97.7% F1-score and 99.9% AUC with Random Forest, while selecting SVM as the most reliable model to avoid overfitting.

- Identified top factors influencing attrition: inadequate incentives, employee age, stress levels, and misaligned monthly income.

Business Impact:

- Predicted a potential 34% increase in attrition over the long term if no preventive actions are taken.

- Proposed actionable strategies including leadership training programs, improved physical work environments, and competitive compensation adjustments.

-Recommended a $3M investment across companies to support junior employee development and long-term business sustainability.
